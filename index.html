Credit Rating Agencies as Central Entities for Bond Market

  1. Historical Success is Trust building mechanism for CRAs.
  2. Government Regulations have made it mandatory for bonds to Rate before issuing.
  3. Market Moves when Ratings Changes which is Trust built by rating agencies, but evidence suggests that market Reactions to Rating Changes May be Due to Regulatory.
  4. As long as their ratings are integrated into the prudential regulatory system, what true vale Maker CRAs bring in Financial Market remains Unresolved.

Questions on Credit Rating Agencies in Market

  1. Cultural and Historical Factors
  2. Issuer Pay Model
  3. Ignorance of Short-Term financial Positions of Company that is likely to reversed

Issuer Pay Model

  1. Entity issuing the bonds (the issuer) pays the CRA for the credit rating
  2. This creates a clear potential conflict of interest, as the issuer could pressure the CRA to give a more favorable rating
  3. As for the Reason of long-term Reputation, CRA maintain their Integrity in Ratings

Alternatives to Issuer-Pay Business Model

  1. Investor Pays or Subscriber Pays Model
  2. Fee Based Service by Advertising Model
  3. Government Mandated Selection model where government assign specific CRA as to issue ratings on random selection basis.

Potential Drawback and Benefits of Increased Competition of Credit Rating Agencies

Dominated By three major CRAs, Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) score : 3655 (10000 equals monopoly and 0 equals Perfect Competition) signify dominance of 3 major CRAs.

  1. Barriers to entry is high, which is believed to be reduced by CRARA,2006.
  2. Competition affect naive investor who rely on ratings due to upwardly biased ratings
  3. If prudential regulatory reliance on ratings were reduced, and professional bond managers were given more flexibility to choose their sources of creditworthiness information, the upward bias from competition might diminish
  4. Although, Increased competition would increase innovation in ratings methodologies, technologies.

Housing Boom before Financial Crisis

  1. Mortgage-Backed securities(MBS) were dominated by government backed firms (Ginnie mac, Fannie Mac, Freddie Mac) that was changed as in late 1990s private label MBS were introduced which contributed to housing boom with increasingly risky loans (subprime mortgages).
  2. MBS were difficult to analyze due to the variety of underlying mortgages and the tranching structure.
  3. MBS were segmented into structures according to different risk level named Tranches, which were divided into senior(Usually AAA rated) and Junior tranches (BB or even Unrated).
  4. Prices of housing declined in 2006-07 causing mortgage default to surge in subprime loans, causing massive loss in junior tranches also senior trances experience shortfalls leading downgrade of MBS.

Role of 3 Major CRAs

  1. CRAs relied on historical data from a rising housing market, which showed low default rates and minimal losses.
  2. Between 2000 and 2007, 3 major CRAs were pivotal in assigning high credit ratings to senior tranches of mortgage-backed securities (MBS). These ratings signaled low risk, facilitating the rapid expansion of the private-label MBS market.
  3. By mid-2007, as mortgage defaults increased, CRAs began large-scale downgrades of MBS and Collateralized debt obligations.
  4. As of mid 2009, 90% of Rated CDOs and 63% of MBS which were rated AAA were downgraded (80% and 52% below of investment grade respectively)

Regulations of Credit Rating Agencies after Financial Crisis

Financial crisis highlighted the failures of CRAs, particularly their rating of mortgage-backed securities (MBS), in Response regulatory efforts are increased.

  1. CRARA (2006): Introduced limited regulation of CRAs, requiring transparency and addressing conflicts of interest.
  2. Dodd-Frank Act (2010): Expanded regulation of CRAs and mandated federal agencies to reduce reliance on NRSRO ratings in prudential regulation.
  3. EU Regulations: Similar efforts were made in the European Union to regulate CRAs.

Drawbacks of Regulations

1. Impact on Smaller CRAs

Regulatory costs, which are often fixed, fall more heavily on smaller CRAs, making it harder for them to compete.

2. Discouragement of Innovation

Stops innovation in rating, as new approaches may not align with regulatory requirements.

3. Entrenchment of Major CRAs

Regulation could further entrench the dominance of the three major CRAs.

Paradigm Shift for Credit Rating Agencies: Is Regulatory Reliance on CRA Mandatory?

Non-Reliance Example of Present Future Actions Can Be taken
Prudential Regulation :Ensuring financial institutions operate in a "safe and sound" manner, and Most Bank Activities such as lending and Deposit doesn't involve Ratings Future shift away from the mandatory reliance on CRA ratings is possible if regulators could adopt a more flexible approach, allowing financial institutions to use a broader range of information sources (including but not limited to CRA ratings) to assess risk

Conclusions and Findings

  1. CRAs are the essential part of the Financial market with three major CRAs have played pivotal role in financial system.
  2. CRAs serve as critical intermediaries in the bond markets, providing creditworthiness assessments that investors rely on.
  3. Their ratings have been integrated into prudential regulation, making them indispensable for regulated financial institutions.

Recommendations

  1. Increased regulation of CRAs, while intended to hold them accountable, may inadvertently entrench their dominance and stifle competition.
  2. Disconnecting regulatory reliance on CRA ratings could foster a more competitive and innovative credit rating industry.
  3. The long-term goal should be to create a resilient and dynamic credit rating industry that serves the needs of the financial system without relying on regulatory mandates.

About CRAs in Nepal

3 Credit Rating Agencies

  1. ICRA NEPAL: A subsidiary of ICRA India, which is linked to Moody's Investors Service. It provides credit ratings for various financial instruments, including IPOs, bank loans, and corporate ratings
  2. CARE Ratings Nepal Limited - Another prominent agency that evaluates credit risk and provides ratings for financial instruments and companies operating in Nepal
  3. Infomerics Credit Rating Nepal Limited - The latest entrant in Nepal's credit rating market. Approved by the Securities Board of Nepal (SEBON) in 2022, this agency provides ratings for bonds, debentures, IPOs, and various bank loan facilities

Ending Statement

  1. The public backlash against CRAs following the financial crisis led to extensive regulation of the industry,
  2. To reduce their influence and hold them accountable decoupling CRA ratings from prudential regulation, policymakers can create a more competitive and innovative credit rating industry that better serves the needs of the financial system
  3. which would not only improve the quality and reliability of creditworthiness assessments but also reduce the risks of over-reliance on a few dominant players.
